  • Brittany — Standard Pass
The team is also registered for the LIONS B2B Summit on June 23. Partner venues (WSJ Journal House, Axios House, Mercedes F1 Yacht, DEPT Secret Garden, etc.) require separate RSVPs beyond the pass.
Badges are collected at the Registration & Badge Collection desk at the Gare Maritime — next door to the Palais des Festivals and the Casino Barrière. Find it on the Map or via the Cannes Lions 2026 app.

Recommended: collect on Sunday 21 June before the festival begins (8:00AM–8:00PM) to avoid Monday morning queues.

Festival Checklist
  • Download the Cannes Lions app once it launches in May — build your personalised agenda and connect with other delegates in advance.
  • Locate your badge barcode in your emails or the app a few days pre-event.
  • Plan to collect your badge on Sunday 21 June during pre-registration (8AM–8PM) to skip Monday queues.
  • Share your experience on social media using #CannesLions2026.
  • Pack a recyclable water bottle — water refill stations are signposted on the Map.
  • Bring a portable charger — your phone will drain fast with the app, navigation, and constant messaging.
  • The Lounge — great for networking. Invite other delegates to meet you there by messaging them directly in the app.
  • First-Timer Tours — scheduled Monday and Tuesday. Search ‘first-timer’ in the app or drop by the Tour Booth by the red carpet stairs. Available in English, French, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Mandarin, Portuguese, and Spanish.
  • Welcome Reception for first-timers — Monday at the Clubhouse Croisette, 1:00PM–2:00PM. Relaxed meet-up with tea, coffee, and mimosas. Sign up via the app in June.
  • Cabana Row — partner companies showcase their work in cabanas; great for networking, meetings, and discovering what partners are building.
  • Daily Networking Meet-Ups — connect with industry legends in interactive sessions and workshops. Find these in the app.
  • The Basement — head downstairs in the Palais to explore work defining innovation and inspiration.
  • Friday Brunch — 10:30AM–1:00PM at the LIONS Creators Beach. Casual wrap-up with fellow delegates, light bites, and relaxed atmosphere.
  • Closing Party — Friday from 9PM–midnight at Carlton Beach. Complimentary drinks and food. (Noted in schedule for Brittany.)
Feeling overwhelmed or just need a place to reset? The Cannes Lions app maps all quiet and shaded spaces at the festival. Sunscreen and sunglasses are essential — there is significant outdoor time between venues. Cannes in late June is typically 25–28°C (77–82°F).

Practical Info
Free Wi-Fi is available onsite throughout the festival. Bring a portable charger — your phone will drain fast with the app running, navigation, and messaging throughout the day.
  • Daytime sessions: Smart casual. Comfortable shoes essential — you’ll cover miles between venues.
  • Yacht/beach events: Smart casual, weather-appropriate. Cannes in June is warm (25–28°C/77–82°F).
  • Evening dinners & award shows: Business smart / cocktail. The Zuma dinners on the Mercedes Yacht are high-value — dress to impress.
Pack sunscreen and sunglasses — significant outdoor time between venues.
Nice Airport (NCE) is 27 km from the Palais. Options:
  • Fixed-rate taxi: ~€85–88, 35–50 min (longer in festival traffic)
  • Helicopter (Blade/Monacair): ~€190–250, 7 minutes, lands at Old Port next to the Palais
  • Regional train: €5–15, ~30 min, Cannes Station is near the Palais
  • Brittany: Arriving 2:40 PM on Sun 21 — allow 60–90 min for baggage and transfer.
  • Brian: Arriving 6:45 PM — allow 60–90 min for baggage and transfer. Avoid A8 peak traffic (4–8 PM).
